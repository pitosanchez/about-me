About

Robert Sanchez

My Journey

I am a 57-year-old Puerto Rican writer, advocate, and visionary from Spanish Harlem who has lived through the fault lines of a health system that too often leaves Black and Brown people behind. After receiving a kidney transplant in 2020, I turned my personal fight for survival into a lifelong mission: transforming how our communities access kidney care, transplant pathways, and the dignity we deserve in medicine.

My lived experience — incarceration, chronic illness, navigating structural racism, rebuilding my life from the ground up — fuels every part of the work I do today. I’ve learned that healing is not just personal; it’s systemic. It demands truth-telling, design, advocacy, and a refusal to accept inequity as normal.

The Work I Do

I am the founder of Kidney Forward and The Jentosy Project, two community-driven initiatives reshaping kidney transplant access for people of color. My work bridges community voices with medical institutions, pushing for systems that respect, protect, and uplift those most harmed by inequity.

I serve on advisory committees, research boards, and national collaborations focused on rare kidney disease, clinical research, and health equity. Whether developing programs, writing commentary, supporting research design, or speaking to audiences, I bring one purpose: build systems where people like me are no longer forced to fight alone.

Why I’m Learning to Code

My coding journey began with a simple belief: technology should serve the communities most affected by injustice. As I build digital tools, websites, data systems, and storytelling platforms, I’m creating infrastructure for advocacy, education, and patient empowerment.

Through The Odin Project and Onramp F25, I’m expanding my engineering skills so I can design and deploy digital spaces that are accessible, medically accurate, and culturally grounded. Coding is not a hobby for me — it’s another way to break down barriers in medicine and make knowledge accessible to the people who need it most.
